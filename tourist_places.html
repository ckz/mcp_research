Famous Tourist Places

Eiffel Tower

Paris, France

The iconic Eiffel Tower, standing at 324 meters tall, is a wrought-iron lattice tower that has become the symbol of both Paris and France. Built in 1889 for the World's Fair, it attracts nearly 7 million visitors annually. The tower offers breathtaking views of the City of Light, with three observation levels and restaurants that provide a unique dining experience above the city.

Grand Canyon

Arizona, United States

The Grand Canyon, carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, is one of nature's most spectacular wonders. Stretching 277 miles long, up to 18 miles wide, and over a mile deep, its layered bands of red rock reveal millions of years of geological history. Visitors can explore numerous viewpoints, hike various trails, or take helicopter tours to experience its magnificent vastness.

Taj Mahal

Agra, India

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum commissioned in 1632 by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife. This UNESCO World Heritage site is considered the finest example of Mughal architecture, combining elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish and Indian architectural styles. The perfectly symmetrical building is surrounded by beautiful gardens and reflecting pools.

Great Wall of China

Beijing, China

The Great Wall of China, stretching over 13,000 miles, is one of humanity's most remarkable architectural achievements. Built over several centuries starting as early as the 7th century BCE, this massive fortification was designed to protect Chinese states from nomadic invasions. Today, it stands as a testament to ancient Chinese engineering and determination, offering visitors stunning views of the surrounding landscape and a walk through history.
